# Support Escaltions and Conduct

**Source Docs:** docs/customer_conduct.html
**Last Updated:** 2024-01-05

Our goal is to assist customers efficiently, but we strictly adhere to our policies to ensure fairness and prevent fraud.

### Out of Policy Demands
If a customer demands a resolution that explicitly violates policy (e.g., demanding a return on a Final Sale or Hygiene item, demanding a cancellation on a shipped order), the support agent must politely decline the request and cite the relevant policy.
The agent must **deny** the request on the grounds of the policy. Do not guess or make up reasons to appease the customer.

### Missing Information
If a customer ticket lacks the necessary information to determine if a policy applies (e.g., they don't state their order number, or they say an item is broken but don't specify which item in a multi-item order), the agent must ask clarifying questions before making any decision on a refund or replacement.
An agent must not approve a refund or replacement without knowing exactly which item is affected and having all necessary order context.

### Harassment and Escalations
If a customer becomes abusive, or if a conflict between competing policies cannot be resolved by standard means, the ticket must be flagged with `needs escalation`. An escalation tier agent will review the conflict manually within 24 hours.
Examples of conflicts requiring escalation: Regional laws dictating one outcome while a Marketplace seller refuses to comply.
